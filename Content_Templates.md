## Contrarian Takes

### Template C1: The Unpopular Opinion

**Structure:**
```

Here's why:

‚Ä¢ [Point 1]
‚Ä¢ [Point 2]
‚Ä¢ [Point 3]

```

**Placeholders:**

- `[Point 1-3]` - Your supporting arguments

**Structure:**

**Placeholders:**

## Numbered Lists

### Template N1: The "X Things" List

**Structure:**
```
[Number] [topic] that [benefit]:

1. [Item] - [brief why]
2. [Item] - [brief why]
3. [Item] - [brief why]
4. [Item] - [brief why]
5. [Item] - [brief why]

Which would you add?
```

**Placeholders:**
- `[Number]` - Odd numbers (5, 7, 9) perform slightly better
- `[topic]` - Specific, not generic
- `[benefit]` - What reader gets from knowing this

**Expected Score:** 70-85
**Best With:** Carousel image of each item
**Avoid:** Generic items everyone knows

**Example:**
```
7 free tools that replaced my $500/month software stack:

1. Notion - project management
2. Canva - design
3. Loom - video recording
4. Calendly - scheduling
5. Mailchimp - email (free tier)
6. Buffer - social scheduling
7. Google Analytics - tracking

Which would you add?
```

## Story Hooks

### Template S1: The Transformation Story

**Structure:**
```
[Time period] ago, I [starting state].

Today, I [ending state].

Here's what changed everything:

[Key insight in 2-3 sentences]

[Call to action question]
```

**Expected Score:** 75-90
**Best With:** Before/after image or screenshot
**Avoid:** Humble bragging without value

**Example:**
```
6 months ago, I had 200 followers and zero engagement.

Today, I crossed 15,000 and my posts regularly hit 100K impressions.

Here's what changed everything:

I stopped posting what I thought was "valuable" and started posting what actually got engagement. I tracked every post for 30 days and doubled down on what worked.

What's stopping you from tracking your content?
```

---

### Template S2: The Failure Story

**Structure:**
```
I [failed at something].

Here's what I learned:

[Lesson 1]
[Lesson 2]
[Lesson 3]

Failure isn't fatal. Quitting is.
```

**Expected Score:** 70-85
**Best With:** Authentic photo or screenshot of the failure
**Avoid:** Fake humility or manufactured failures

---

### Template S3: The "I Was Wrong" Admission

**Structure:**

```
I used to believe [old belief].

Then [what happened].

```

**Expected Score:** 65-80
**Best With:** Text-only works well

## Engagement Polls

### Template P1: The "This or That" Poll

**Structure:**
```
[Context question]

```

**Poll Options:**
- Option A
- Option B
- Both
- Neither

**Expected Score:** 75-85
**Best With:** Relevant image

## Using Templates Effectively

### Do:
- Customize every placeholder with specific details
- Add your unique voice and perspective
- Test variations to find what works for YOUR audience
- Track performance by template type

### Don't:
- Use templates verbatim without customization
- Use the same template repeatedly
- Choose templates that don't fit your content
- Ignore what the data tells you about performance
